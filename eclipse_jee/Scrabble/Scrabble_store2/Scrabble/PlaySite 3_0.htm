   Setup
 

The game will automatically distribute the first seven tiles to each player. The player who created the new table plays first, other players play in the order they joined the table.

   Game play
 

The first player combines two or more of his or her letters to form a word, then places the word on the board to read either across or down and with one letter on the center square. Diagonal words are not allowed.

Words can be placed by dragging the tiles to the appropriate squares. They can also be placed by clicking the first square of the proposed word. An arrow pointing to the right appears; a second click changes to an arrow pointing down. Once the direction of the word is determined, the player can type his or her letters.

Play then passes to the next listed player. That player, and then each in turn, adds one or more letters to those already on the board to form new words. All letters played on a turn must be placed in one row across to form at least one word from left to right, or down to form at least one word from top to bottom. If the new letters also touch other letters in adjacent rows, they must also form complete words across and/or down.

New words may be formed by:

  • Adding one or more letters to a word already on the board.
  • Placing a word at right angles to a word already on the board. The new word must use one of the letters already on the board or must add a letter to it.
  • Placing a complete word parallel to a word already played so that the adjacent letters also form complete words.

When a word is completed, the player's score will be posted in the window to the right.

New tiles appear on the player's rack to return his or her total to seven (as long as there are enough tiles left in the bag). The current number of tiles remaining in the bag is posted in the window to the right. No tile may be shifted or replaced after it has been played and scored.

   Blanks
 

The two blank tiles are "wild cards," and may be used to represent any letter needed to complete a word. When playing a blank, the player must indicate which letter it represents. The letter is shown in red, and it remains that letter for the rest of the game. Blank tiles have no point value, regardless of which letter they may stand for.

 Exchanging tiles
 

You may use a turn to exchange any or all of your tiles. To do this, choose the tiles you want replaced and click Exchange. This ends your turn.

   Passing
 

If you are unable to form a word with the tiles in your rack, you may choose to Pass by clicking the Pass button. Play by an opponent may enable you to use those tiles on your next turn. If you wish to end the game with tiles still left in your rack, a pass by each player will end the game and the final score will be shown.

   Challenges
 

The game automatically refers to a built-in version of The Official SCRABBLE Players Dictionary, Third Edition. All words labeled as a part of speech (including those listed of foreign origin, and as archaic, obsolete, colloquial, slang, etc.) are permitted, with the exception of the following:

  • Words that are typically capitalized, such as cities, states or days of the week.
  • Abbreviations, prefixes and suffixes standing alone.
  • Words requiring a hyphen or an apostrophe.

Any play may be challenged before the next player starts a turn by clicking on the Challenge flag. If the play challenged is unacceptable, the challenged player gets back his or her tiles and loses that turn. If the play challenged is acceptable, the challenger loses his or her next turn. All words formed in one play are challenged simultaneously. If any word is unacceptable, the entire play is unacceptable. Only one turn is lost on any challenge.

   Scoring
 

The game automatically tallies each player's score and displays it after each turn. The score value of each letter is indicated by a number at the bottom of the tile. The score value of a blank is zero.

The score for each turn is the sum of the letter values in each word(s) formed or modified on that turn, plus the additional points obtained from placing letters on premium squares.

Letter and word premiums count only on the turn in which they are first played. On later turns, letters already played on premium squares count at face value.

  • Premium Letter Squares: A light blue square doubles the score of a letter placed on it; a dark blue square triples the letter score.

  • Premium Word Squares: The score for an entire word is doubled when one of its letters is placed on a pink square; it is tripled when one of its letters is placed on a red square. Include premiums for double or triple letter values, if any, before doubling or tripling the word score. If a word covers two premium word squares, the score is doubled and then re-doubled (4 times the letter count), or tripled and then re-tripled (9 times the letter count).

Note: The center square is a pink square, which doubles the score for the first word.

When a blank tile is played on a pink or red square, the value of the word is doubled or tripled, even though the blank itself has no score value.

When two or more words are formed in the same play, each is scored. The common letter is counted (with full premium value, if applicable) for each word.

If you play seven tiles on a turn, you've made a bingo, and you add a premium of 50 points to your score for that turn.

   And the winner is
 

The game ends when all letters have been drawn and one player uses his or her last letter, or when all possible plays have been made.

Each player's total score for the game is reduced by the sum of his or her unplayed letters. In addition, if a player used all of his or her letters, the sum of the other players' unplayed letters is added to that player's score.

The player with the highest final score wins the game. In case of a tie, the player with the highest score before adding or deducting unplayed letters wins.
